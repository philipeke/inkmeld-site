This Privacy Policy explains in detail how InkMeld and OakDev & AI AB collect, use, store, protect, transfer, disclose, and delete personal data connected to the InkMeld website, mobile app, support channels, billing flows, AI image-processing features, and related business operations.

This notice is intentionally detailed. It is designed to describe what the service does today, what may happen when optional features are turned on, and what rights you may have depending on where you live. If mandatory law gives you stronger rights than this policy, that law controls.

1. Privacy At A Glance

InkMeld is an AI-powered tattoo preview service. It lets you import or capture body photos, import tattoo designs, generate cutouts, place artwork on body images, tune realism, export previews, and optionally sync certain library data between devices. Most creative content can remain on your device unless and until you choose a feature that needs cloud processing, synchronization, support, diagnostics, billing validation, advertising, or analytics.

  • By default, the app creates a Firebase user session so credits, purchases, and security controls can work. That session may begin anonymously and later be linked to Google or Apple.
  • If you use AI cutout or background removal, the image you choose is sent through the InkMeld backend and to configured AI-processing providers so the requested output can be generated.
  • If you use cloud sync, body photos, tattoo source files, cutouts, and certain preset metadata may be uploaded to Firebase Storage and Firestore under your user account.
  • If you make purchases, Apple or Google process the payment and InkMeld may verify receipts or purchase tokens server-side before credits or premium entitlements are granted.
  • If you use the free tier, the app may request consent for advertising and tracking-related permissions and may show personalized or non-personalized ads where legally and technically permitted.
  • If you use the website, you may choose to contact us by email, open a deletion request, or read legal pages. Website analytics tooling may also be enabled in some deployments, subject to applicable cookie and consent rules.

The service is built around photos of real bodies and tattoo ideas. That means user-submitted images can sometimes reveal more than a standard profile picture. For example, a body photo may show scars, skin conditions, disability indicators, surgery marks, religious or political symbols, ethnic or racial traits, intimate body areas, or other information that could be sensitive under some laws.

If you choose to submit images that contain sensitive information, please only submit what is necessary for your purpose. 9. AI Image Processing And Background Removal

InkMeld includes AI-assisted cutout functionality. When you intentionally trigger that feature, the image file you selected is sent to an InkMeld backend endpoint together with security credentials such as an authenticated user token and, where supported, a Firebase App Check token. The backend may record limited usage metadata, such as the request timestamp, status, month key, byte counts, and the charging source used for that operation.

Based on the current code reviewed for this policy, the AI cutout path works broadly as follows:

  • the app sends the selected image bytes to an InkMeld backend endpoint;
  • the backend verifies authorization and App Check;
  • the backend logs or updates your remaining quota or purchased credits;
  • the backend passes the submitted image to a configured AI-processing provider for background removal;
  • the returned output is post-processed, which may include trimming, tattoo-ink extraction, cropping, or related image refinement; and
  • the resulting PNG output is returned to the app.

The code reviewed for the current app stack uses Replicate as an AI-processing provider for background removal. Replicate's public documentation states that API prediction input parameters, output values, output files, and logs are automatically removed after about one hour by default, but third-party retention and internal infrastructure logs may still vary. We do not control the independent privacy practices of outside providers and may change providers over time. For that reason, please avoid sending unnecessary personal or highly sensitive data through AI features.

If an AI request fails after charging logic has started, the backend may attempt to refund the relevant quota or purchased credit. Operational records about failed or refunded requests may still be retained for security, support, reconciliation, or abuse-prevention reasons.

10. Anonymous Sessions, Linked Accounts, And Authentication

The current InkMeld app is designed to sign users into Firebase at startup. If you have not created or linked a named account, the app may create an anonymous Firebase-authenticated session so that credits, quotas, purchases, and cloud controls can still function. That means we may still hold a stable backend user ID even if you never create a password-based account.

If you later choose to link your session with Google or Apple, we may receive or create additional identity data such as an email address, provider identifier, and account-linking events. If Apple provides a private relay email, that relay address may become the contact point associated with the linked account. Depending on the authentication provider and platform, names or display information may also be shared.

Linking a session can help preserve access to entitlements across devices or re-installs. If you remain anonymous and then lose the session, replace the device, or uninstall the app before linking, recovery may be limited because the entitlements may still be associated with the old Firebase user ID rather than a named account.

If you use cloud sync, the app may upload the following categories of content to Firebase services associated with your account:

  • original tattoo source files;
  • generated cutout files;
  • body photos;
  • editor preset metadata;
  • remote storage paths and sync version data;

If you delete local content but do not sync that deletion, a remote copy may remain available until the deletion has been propagated or another cleanup process occurs.

Where cloud data is restored to a device, the app may recreate local files from Firebase Storage into app-local folders. This means copies can exist locally, remotely, or both, depending on your actions.

35. Data Minimization, Need-To-Know Access, And Privacy By Design

We aim to follow data-minimization principles by trying not to collect more personal data than is reasonably needed for the function, support task, legal obligation, or security purpose at issue. In practice, that means different features may collect different data sets, and not every field, permission, or upload path is required for every user.

Internally, access to personal data should be limited on a need-to-know basis. Not every employee, contractor, or service provider has the same level of access. Access may depend on role, technical necessity, security responsibility, customer-support assignment, or legal/compliance duties. We may also use role separation, logging, audit review, pseudonymization, or environment controls to reduce unnecessary exposure.

Privacy by design does not mean zero risk or zero data processing. It means we try to design features, workflows, and provider choices with privacy and security in mind from the start and to revisit those choices as the product evolves.
