The Workflow

From random image to
body-ready preview.

Four steps. Simple enough to feel immediate, deep enough to feel powerful.

01

Import or photograph

Start with a body photo you shot yourself. Bring in the tattoo source from a screenshot, design, symbol, sketch, or reference. Any source.

02

Run the AI cutout

InkMeld isolates the exact motif you care about — not the background, not a screenshot, the object itself, clean and ready.

03

Place and sculpt

Move, rotate, scale, tint, blend, soften, and tune how the ink sits against skin until the illusion feels locked in.

04

Save, export, share

When the preview hits, keep it. Save to your account, export it, share it with your artist, or compare directions before you book.

FAQ

Questions before letting the future
near your skin.

The goal is not to replace tattoo artists. The goal is to arrive at the artist with far better signal.

Can I use an image from the internet as a starting point?

Yes. InkMeld is built for reference chaos. Import a web image, isolate the object with AI, then move into the preview editor to see how that exact visual behaves on your own body photo.

What can I adjust once the tattoo is placed?

Placement, scale, rotation, tint, brightness, contrast, saturation, hue, opacity, edge softness, micro blur, grain, shadow, highlight, and skin blend — twelve axes of control across color and realism.

Is the preview only for black ink?

No. InkMeld includes tint handling and multiple material presets that push the preview toward dense blackwork, softer ink behavior, futuristic neon energy, or hard reflective finishes.

How do credits work?

The app supports a monthly quota plus purchased AI credits. Commerce flows use server-side receipt verification so credit grants happen only after successful validation.

Can I save or share my result with an artist?

Yes. Save, export, and share behavior lets your best previews move from experiment to real conversation — ready to use in artist consultations or side-by-side comparison rounds.

Who is InkMeld for?

Anyone in the tattoo decision chain: first-timers exploring ideas, collectors planning their next piece, professional artists streamlining client consultations, and studios that want a modern premium experience.
